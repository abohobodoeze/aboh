A Brief History On Aboh Kingdom

With supporting text below as a natural lead-in to additional content.

Abohs are Benins by origin. At about 1405 A.D, Obazome alias Esumai, the son of Oba Ozolua of Benin immigrated with some of his followers from Benin as a result of dispute in the Benin palace after his father had vacated the throne by death, to plant Aboh town.

During the immigration, the immigrants mer Iwelle group, who also immigrated from Itsekiri, between Ashaka and Igbuku land where thier native god (ogilishi tree) can be traced today. When Obazome and his group met the Iwelles and requested the Iwelles to cross them to the other side of Ase creek leading to the river Niger, the Iwelles agreed with I'll intension. By the time of crossing the Iwelles found it possible to kill some of the Benin immigrants as the Iwelles understood that the immigrants did not know how to swim. After the immigrants have embarked on the canoe the Iwelles pull out into the midstream and say to themselves "GBAMI YIOKO" meaning capsize the canoe. This action was severally done and so many of the Benins died by drowning.

The Benins however found means to cross the Ase river and from there they found thier way and got to Northern end of Aboh very close to the river Niger bank known today as "ObodoUku" the present burial ground. By the time they came in they met the Akarai people at the southern end of the present Aboh. After a while the two groups began to struggle for leadership which resulted to war . The war was in favour of the Benins. Akarai group packed out. Some went Northward of the Niger and planted Akarai ogidi while others went into the bush South of Aboh town. Later both groups settled their differences and Akarai group had a representative in the Aboh palace known as "Oghene".

Obazome gave the eldest Akara (Okpalauku) the right in the palace not to kneel down when greeting the OBI of Aboh. Oghene still stands today in Aboh palace and known as Ogene.
